AI SEO TOOL — CLIENT PRODUCT GUIDE (PLAIN ENGLISH)
==================================================

Who this is for: business clients using the platform (not developers).

What this document covers: how the product helps you, what each paid plan
includes, step-by-step usage for Starter / Growth / Elite, how keyword
suggestions work, who does what between you and your agency, and short
definitions of terms. Updated March 2026.

Visual flowcharts exist as separate files in the same client-guide folder:
flowchart-starter.svg, flowchart-growth.svg, flowchart-elite.svg. Open those in
a browser or image viewer; they are not embedded in this text file.


1. WHAT THIS TOOL DOES (SIMPLE EXPLANATION)
-------------------------------------------

The AI SEO Tool helps you understand how your website is performing, see what
people type into Google before they find you (when you connect Google Search
Console), and track important keywords next to real clicks and rankings.

It uses a “business profile” — your industry, goals, and keywords — so AI audits
and suggestions sound like your company, not a generic template.

Depending on your plan you may also get AI blog drafts, a backlink checklist,
and on the top plan a social ads area for Meta/Instagram, usually with help
from your agency.


2. PLAN COMPARISON — WHAT EACH TIER INCLUDES
--------------------------------------------

All plans include:
• Dashboard overview and business profile.
• Industry vertical and marketing goal presets (they shape AI outputs).
• Site audit (AI recommendations plus Lighthouse-style scores when configured).
• Site crawl (multi-page, within app limits).
• Search performance via Google Search Console (after you connect Google).
• Keywords with optional match to Search Console data — limits differ by plan:
  Starter up to 10, Growth up to 50, Elite up to 100.
• Audit progress card comparing your first saved audit to your latest.
• Unified growth score on Overview with a short breakdown.
• Competitor homepage watchlist with snapshot diff:
  Starter 1 URL, Growth 3, Elite 5.
• Lead-magnet style blocks from the site audit.
• Social proof lines (needs two audits with measurable movement).
• On-page checklist per URL.
• Site audit extras: CTA/forms, FAQ, objections, lead capture blocks.

Monthly executive email:
• Starter: not included.
• Growth: opt-in, HTML summary (server needs email provider + cron).
• Elite: opt-in, HTML plus optional PDF attachment when configured.

AI blogs:
• Starter: not included.
• Growth: yes, with a weekly limit set on your subscription.
• Elite: yes.

Backlink directory checklist:
• Starter: no.
• Growth and Elite: yes.

Content calendar:
• Starter: up to 2 items.
• Growth and Elite: large capacity (hundreds).

Social caption packs:
• Starter: from audit, LinkedIn only.
• Growth and Elite: from audit and from blog posts — LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook.

Social ads (Meta / Instagram workflow):
• Starter and Growth: not included.
• Elite: included.

Your agency turns plans on and may set blog/backlink limits. Search Console
needs your Google account and a verified property for your site.


3. STARTER PLAN — STEP BY STEP
------------------------------

For businesses that want audits, crawl, search queries, and keyword tracking
without the full content and backlink bundle.

Typical order of work:

1) You get login details after the admin creates your account on Starter.

2) Log in at the app URL.

3) Business profile: enter website URL, business name, industry text, target
   keywords (comma-separated), industry vertical, and marketing goal. Save.
   This tunes the AI and keyword suggestions.

4) Site audit: run on your main URL; read findings, Lighthouse categories, and
   AI blocks. Run again later to compare (see Overview).

5) Site crawl: optional broader health check from a start URL.

6) Search performance: connect Google, pick the property that matches your site,
   sync. You see roughly the last 28 days of queries (read-only access).

7) Keywords: review suggested terms within your 10-keyword cap; edit or export.

8) Overview: audit summary, audit progress (first vs latest), growth score.

9) Competitors: add competitor homepages; refresh to see title/meta/H1 changes.

10) Content calendar: add a few items or seed from audit pillars. Starter allows
    only two items total.

11) On-page checklist: enter URL, save checklist, tick tasks as you fix them.

12) Audit extras: copy CTA, FAQ, objection, lead capture, lead-magnet blocks,
    and social proof lines when you have enough audit history.

13) Social captions from audit: generate; on Starter you get LinkedIn text only.


4. GROWTH PLAN — EVERYTHING ABOVE, PLUS
---------------------------------------

9) Blogs: open Blogs; agency or automation may generate drafts. Stay within
    your weekly blog limit shown on the dashboard.

10) Backlinks: work the directory checklist; mark submitted/verified. Overview
    may show batch progress.

11) Keywords: up to 50; use Refresh suggestions after profile or GSC updates.

12) Social post pack on a blog: generate LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook from a post.

13) Monthly executive email: opt in under Business profile if your operator
    enabled it. Elite adds PDF on the same email when configured.


5. ELITE PLAN — EVERYTHING ABOVE, PLUS
--------------------------------------

12) Social ads: connect Meta/Instagram credentials in the UI where allowed;
    review drafts; publishing may still need extra Meta Business setup.

13) Keywords: up to 100.

14) Monthly email can include a one-page PDF brief for Elite when enabled.


6. HOW AUTOMATIC KEYWORD SUGGESTIONS WORK
-----------------------------------------

• Suggestions come from: target keywords field, industry text, business name,
  vertical label, and “content pillar” ideas from your latest site audit.

• After Search Console syncs, top queries can fill remaining slots up to your plan cap.

• New suggestions add rows; they do not blindly delete your edits. Refresh
  suggestions merges new ideas up to the cap.

• If you edit a keyword’s text and save, it is treated as yours (manual) so the
  app keeps your wording in mind.


7. ROLES — YOU VS AGENCY
------------------------

Plan and billing: you use what your plan includes; agency assigns the plan and
may generate blogs or social drafts for you.

Business data: you edit the business profile anytime; agency can change fields if you ask.

Google Search Console: you connect your own Google account (read-only); agency may help verify the property.

Audits and crawl: you run them and read results; agency may advise on fixes outside the app.


8. PHASE 4 IDEAS IN ONE PARAGRAPH
---------------------------------

The growth score blends lab SEO, opportunity, momentum between audits, checklist
(and blog cadence on Growth+), minus a small penalty for serious open issues.
Competitors store homepage snapshots you can refresh. Lead-magnet and social
proof text reuse audit output. The monthly email summarizes the dashboard; Elite
can attach a PDF.


9. PHASE 3 IDEAS IN ONE PARAGRAPH
---------------------------------

Calendar, on-page checklist, and social packs turn audits into weekly work.
Extended audit blocks add CTA, FAQ, objection, and lead capture copy on every run.


10. GLOSSARY
------------

Lighthouse / lab scores — Automated scores for performance, SEO, accessibility,
and best practices from Google’s tooling pipeline inside the product.

Search Console — Google’s view of queries that showed your site; we import
recent aggregates after you authorize read-only access.

Tracked keyword — A phrase you track; we try to match it to a Search Console
query to show clicks, impressions, and average position.

Before / after (audit progress) — Compares your earliest saved audit to your
newest to show change over time.

Growth score (version 1) — Weighted mix of latest lab SEO, opportunity, trajectory
between audits, checklist completion, and (on Growth+) blog cadence, with a small
deduction for unresolved high-severity findings.

Competitor snapshot — Stored title, meta description, and H1 count from a
competitor URL; refreshing replaces it and shows a diff from the previous capture.
